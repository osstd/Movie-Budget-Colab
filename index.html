Movie Budget and Financial Performance Records

Introduction

Do higher film budgets lead to more box office revenue? Let's find out if there's a relationship using the movie budgets and financial performance data that have been scraped from the-numbers.com on May 1st, 2018.

Seaborn Regression Plots Old Films

Create a DataFrame for old films that includes all the films released up to 1969, and create another DataFrame called new films that includes all the films released from 1970 onwards. These two DataFrames will help in analyzing the films before and after 1970 separately.

This following plot shows the relationship between worldwide gross and production budget for films released up to 1969 (old films). The plot reveals a positive correlation between the two variables, indicating that films with higher production budgets tend to have higher worldwide grosses. Notably, the most expensive film produced during this period was Cleopatra, with a budget of 42 million USD.

Figure 3

New Films

This plot shows the relationship between worldwide gross and production budget for films released from 1970 onwards (new films). The data points for new films demonstrate a stronger linear correlation compared to old films, indicating a more predictable relationship between budget and success. Notably, Avatar (released in 2009) holds the record for the highest worldwide gross, approximately 2.8 billion USD, as of May 2018. According to the regression, a film with a production cost of 100 million USD would be expected to gross around 300 million USD, compared to approximately 190 million USD for old films.
